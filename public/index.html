Jonathan Sharpe

This is me.

Sadly, though, that is not my puppy; my brother took the photo on a trip to the Tromsø Wilderness Centre.

I am a (reasonably) professional transport consultant and software developer and a (very) amateur guitarist and photographer.

Bracknell

For the last few years I've been living in Bracknell, UK:

Senior Researcher

Transport Research Laboratory

My primary responsibilities are as a research lead, working autonomously to deliver innovative projects, and as a developer of software tools for decision support and asset modelling. I also manage teams to deliver more complex projects and, as an associate fellow of the TRL Academy, I contribute to the development and delivery of TRL’s scientific goals, including leading a project on “big data”. Significant projects have included a technology horizons scan for the Department for Transport, which produced a published report and presentations to the TRL Academy and the DfT's Chief Scientific Advisor's technical seminar; leading a team to design and deliver a multi-tier .NET web application for Highways England; taking a leading role in the development of a complex financial model for the DfT, which is currently undergoing external assurance; and the development of a probabilistic whole- life costing technique for Shell.

My research skills include: development of new data processing techniques and value management methodologies; and analysis of both the broad technological issues surrounding transportation generally and management of its infrastructure assets specifically. Recent involvement in EU research projects (SmartRAIL, Capacity4Rail and DestinationRail) has given me greater experience of working with large, international, interdisciplinary teams.

In terms of software development Python is my go-to language, but I have experience with the .NET stack (largely VB, including WPF for desktop apps and ASP Web Forms with SQL Server for web applications) and Excel/VBA, too. My experience covers the whole software lifecycle, from initial requirements capture, through design and development to testing, deployment and support, and I’ve been involved in the validation of commercial software, from technical and business requirement standpoints.
